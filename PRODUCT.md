## Users

Four Malaysian segments from the NEXTGEN market-segmentation deck:

1. **Insurance Novice** — 18–35, students/fresh grads/early-career, B40–M40, Klang Valley/JB urban. Digital natives intimidated by jargon; first-time buyers. Mobile-first, transact entirely in-app.
2. **Independent Professional** — 20–45 gig workers (Grab, FoodPanda, Lalamove), variable income, road-hazard exposed. Need flexible micro-coverage they can pause/adjust seasonally.
3. **High-Utilization User** — 30–60 families/business owners, M40–T20, multi-asset (motor, home, family medical, critical illness). Want one dashboard, redundancy removal, renewals, optimization.
4. **Global Resident** — expats/digital nomads facing unfamiliar local systems, language barriers, scam risk. Need transparent recommendations and instant digital purchase.

Context of use: evenings, on a phone or laptop, after work/shifts; a stressful, low-trust purchase the user has been putting off.

## Product Purpose

NEXTINSURANCE (by NEXTGEN Co.) is an AI-powered insurance recommender that removes guesswork from choosing coverage. The user states preferences and a monthly RM budget; the AI guide **NEXTAdvisor** scans partnered providers and returns a ranked list of personalized **Top Matches** with a transparent Match Score; the user compares and purchases in-app. It attacks three market failures: **Complexity** (dense legal jargon), **Agent Bias** (commission-driven selling), and **Analysis Paralysis** (hundreds of undifferentiated plans). Success = a user goes from "no idea" to a confident, explainable plan choice in under 3 minutes.

## Brand Personality

Precise, reassuring, forward. A calm financial cockpit, not a sales floor. The interface should feel like an instrument that works *for* the user — evidence-first (scores, breakdowns, plain-language decoding), never pushy. Tagline voice: "Complex coverage, simplified by AI."

## Anti-references

- BJAK / RinggitPlus comparison-site clutter: banner ads, 40-row tables, "BEST DEAL!!" badges, popups.
- Generic dark SaaS landing page: purple gradient, glassmorphism cards everywhere, hero-metric template.
- Traditional insurer corporate sites: stock photos of smiling families, trust-by-beige, PDF-first.
- Aggressive fintech FOMO (countdown timers, fake scarcity) — directly contradicts the bias-free promise.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the why, not just the what.** Every recommendation carries its reasons (score breakdown, plain-language rationale). Trust is earned by transparency, not asserted.
2. **One decision per screen.** The product exists to end analysis paralysis; never present more simultaneous choices than the user can hold in mind.
3. **Decode, don't dumb down.** Jargon gets translated alongside the original term, never hidden — users should leave more literate, not more dependent.
4. **Calm under money stress.** Motion conveys state (scanning, scoring, confirming), never decoration on app surfaces; numbers are stable, legible, tabular.
5. **Bias-free means visually neutral ranking.** No paid-placement visual tricks; rank order = score order, and the score math is inspectable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA: ≥4.5:1 body contrast on the dark theme, visible focus states, full keyboard path through onboarding → purchase, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for every animation, multilingual-ready copy (EN at prototype stage; BM/中文 planned for Global Resident segment).
